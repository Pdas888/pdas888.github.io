Research

1. Reverse-shocked ejecta and the double-shell morphology

Using deep MUSE observations of the Type Ia supernova remnant SNR 0509–67.5, I discovered a remarkable double-shell [Ca XV] structure in the reverse-shocked ejecta. This layered morphology — with sulphur emission enclosed between two calcium shells — provides the first photographic evidence of the double-detonation explosion mechanism. It directly links observed ejecta geometry to theoretical sub-Chandrasekhar explosion models, marking a significant step in connecting explosion physics to remnant morphology.

2. Helium emission lines as diagnostic tools

I identified both broad and narrow He I and He II lines across multiple Type Ia remnants in the LMC using archival MUSE data. This represents the first spatially resolved detection of helium in such environments. By mapping He/H ratios across the shock zones, I developed a diagnostic method to constrain the pre-shock neutral helium fraction — revealing new information about the circumstellar medium and progenitor system composition.

3. Spatially resolved clumps and 3D model comparison

I performed a detailed analysis of clump substructures within the reverse-shocked ejecta, combining imaging and spectroscopy to resolve small-scale density enhancements. These observed clumps were compared with 3D hydrodynamical models (e.g. from Ferrand et al. 2022), enabling one-to-one matching of the observed morphology with simulated ejecta distributions. This work provides new insight into the role of asymmetries, instabilities, and composition gradients in the shaping of Type Ia remnants.

4. Machine-learning tools for spectral decomposition

To efficiently analyze tens of thousands of spectra from MUSE data cubes, I developed a supervised machine-learning (ML) framework capable of detecting and fitting broad emission components automatically. The model classifies spectral profiles and performs Gaussian decomposition, enabling 3D reconstruction of the ejecta velocity structure. This ML-based pipeline establishes a reproducible, scalable framework for studying spectroscopic data in supernova remnants and beyond.
